# Swaya 12-Month Revenue Projections (Source-Available Model)

This document provides a month-by-month financial projection for Swaya's first year under the Source-Available / No-Redistribution licensing model.

## Core Assumptions & Pricing Strategy
To model these projections, we assume the following price points (highly competitive for personal media managers):
* **Monthly Subscription:** $3 / month (convenience, updates)
* **Annual Subscription:** $20 / year (~45% savings)
* **Lifetime License:** $49 (one-time purchase)
* **Assumed Mix of Purchases:** 50% Lifetime, 30% Monthly, 20% Annual.
* **Average Initial Revenue per Customer (ARPU):** ~$29 (weighted average of initial purchases).

## Scenario Analysis

### 1. Pessimistic Scenario (Slow growth, low conversion)
* **Marketing:** Occasional Reddit posts, very low engagement. Hungarian communities are interested but hesitant to buy.
* **Conversion Rate:** 0.5% (only 1 out of 200 visitors buys a license).
* **Traffic Growth:** Starts at 200 visits/month in M1, growing by 15-20% monthly to 1,500 visits/month in M12.
* **Outcome:** The app generates a small side income ($520/month by M12). It serves as a proof of concept but doesn't fully cover the opportunity cost of your time.

### 2. Realistic Scenario (Steady growth, solid conversion)
* **Marketing:** Active engagement in r/selfhosted and media manager subreddits. Snitt.hu integration is completed, drawing local movie collectors. Word-of-mouth begins to drive organic traffic.
* **Conversion Rate:** 1.5% (typical for niche utility apps with high-value core features).
* **Traffic Growth:** Starts at 500 visits/month in M1, growing steadily by 25% monthly to 6,000 visits/month in M12.
* **Outcome:** You reach a sustainable side business ($6,000/month by M12, totaling $26k in Year 1). This justifies dedicating significant weekly development hours and pays for infrastructure.

### 3. Optimistic Scenario (Viral adoption, high conversion)
* **Marketing:** Launches generate major hype on Reddit/HackerNews. Integration with local communities like Snitt.hu hits a sweet spot. Power users actively promote Swaya because of its local-first privacy focus and superior UI.
* **Conversion Rate:** 3.0% (driven by excellent UX, fast release cycles, and strong premium feature incentives).
* **Traffic Growth:** Starts at 1,000 visits/month, growing rapidly to 20,000 visits/month by M12.
* **Outcome:** The project turns into a highly profitable business ($23k/month by M12, totaling over $100k in Year 1). This allows you to work on Swaya full-time.

## Growth Acceleration Tips for Solo Developers
* **Integrate Local Communities early:** Snitt.hu users are highly active and lack custom desktop tools. A direct API integration makes Swaya the "default choice" for them.
* **Emphasize the Source-Available / Privacy angle:** Communities like Reddit's `r/selfhosted` hate closed-source cash-grabs, but they *highly respect* developers who share their source code under a non-redistribution license. It builds trust instantly.
* **Focus Premium on Convenience:** Keep the local database/scrapers free to run from source, but charge for auto-updates, cloud backup integrations, and multi-device sync.
